## 1. Portrait Composition Rules (9:16)

Facebook Reels is a vertical-first format. Every image and video must be composed with this in mind from the prompt level.

### Core Principles

- **Fill the vertical frame** â€” the subject should occupy most of the height. Avoid wide horizontal compositions that leave dead space on sides.
- **Center the main subject** â€” faces, figures, and focal points belong at or near the vertical center. Avoid hard left or right anchoring.
- **Use vertical motion and elements** â€” lightning striking downward, serpents rising upward, waterfalls cascading, light beams cutting from top to bottom. These naturally guide the eye and feel dynamic in portrait.
- **Sky above, ground below** â€” compositions that use the full vertical range (sky in the upper third, ground or water in the lower third) read beautifully in Reels.
- **Avoid ultra-wide landscapes** â€” a mountain range that stretches horizontally reads as wasted space in portrait. Reframe as a single towering peak filling the frame.

### Safe Zones for Text Overlays

Facebook Reels UI elements eat into the frame:

| Zone | Usage |
|---|---|
| Top 10% | Avoid â€” Facebook UI bar |
| Top 10â€“25% | Hook text / title overlay safe zone |
| Middle 50% | Primary visual content |
| Bottom 20â€“35% | Caption preview, CTA buttons, profile info |
| Bottom 10% | Avoid â€” navigation bar |

Keep the most visually important content in the **middle 50%** of the frame. Text overlays added in post should sit in the top 15â€“25% zone.

## 2. Hook Writing (First 3 Seconds Are Everything)

Facebook's algorithm rewards watch time. If viewers drop off in the first 3 seconds, the Reel will not be pushed. The hook is the single most important element of any Reel.

### Hook Frameworks

**Curiosity Gap** â€” hint at something without revealing it:
- "She hid her light... and the world went dark."
- "The gods made a deal they could never undo."

**Dramatic Statement** â€” open with the most extreme fact:
- "He stole fire from the godsâ€¦ and paid forever."
- "Every single night, a god fights to bring you the sunrise."

**Question Hook** â€” pull the viewer into a question they want answered:
- "What happens when the sun god loses?"
- "Which Norse god would you want fighting beside you?"

**Number Hook** â€” specificity creates credibility:
- "3 minutes before the gods decided humanity's fate."
- "One mistake. One punishment. 10,000 years."

### Hook Rules

- **Keep it under 7 words** if possible â€” it must be readable in 1â€“2 seconds as an overlay
- **Never start with "I"** â€” algorithm de-prioritizes first-person openers on Reels
- **Make it incomplete** â€” the hook should raise a question the video answers
- **Match the visual** â€” the hook text should describe exactly what the viewer sees in frame 1

## 6. Audio Recommendations Per Content Type

Audio is the second most important factor (after the hook) for Reel performance. Facebook heavily promotes Reels that use trending audio.

### ghibli_mythology

- Epic orchestral with ethnic instruments (Nordic, Japanese, Egyptian flavors matching the myth)
- Builds from quiet ambient to dramatic swell at the climax moment
- Recommended approach: layer soft ambient music under narration or silence in the first 3s, then bring in music at full at the 4s mark
- Avoid: upbeat pop, lo-fi beats, anything tonally mismatched

### realistic_mythology

- Heavy orchestral, dramatic strings, distant thunder
- Cinematic silence followed by a single percussive hit works extremely well as a hook audio technique
- Avoid: any music that reads as "cute" or "playful" â€” it kills the serious tone

### nature_ambient

- Soft ambient only â€” gentle nature sounds (rain, forest, wind), subtle piano or koto
- No drums, no beat drops, no rhythm
- Pure ambient music or nature SFX loops work best
- Facebook rewards long watch times on ambient content â€” peaceful audio keeps viewers watching on loop

## 7. Engagement Tips

### Reply to Comments Quickly

Facebook's algorithm treats comment replies as engagement signals. Replying within the first 60 minutes of posting dramatically increases reach. Even short replies ("Which myth is next? ðŸ‘€") re-engage commenters.

### Use Polls and Questions

Facebook Reels supports sticker-style interaction. A simple poll ("Thor or Odin?") in a mythology Reel can triple comment activity. Add polls to every mythology post where possible.

### Pin a Comment

Pin your own comment with a follow-up question or additional story context. This gives viewers something to engage with even after reading the caption.

### Cross-Post to Facebook Groups

After posting, manually share the Reel to relevant Facebook Groups (listed in each `story_info.json` under `suggested_groups`). Group shares are the fastest way to cold-start reach on new accounts.

### Respond to Saves

"Save rate" (saves per view) is the metric Facebook values most for long-term distribution. Encourage saves explicitly: "Save this â€” you'll want to rewatch it."

## 9. Portrait Prompt Writing Tips for Flux 2 Klein and LTX-2

Writing good portrait prompts is different from standard image generation prompts. These tips apply to all content in this pipeline.

### Always Open With the Style Anchor

Every prompt should begin with the style definition before describing the scene. This anchors the model's style processing before it generates composition.

Examples:
- `Studio Ghibli anime watercolor illustration, portrait orientation,`
- `Hyper-realistic cinematic oil painting, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting, portrait orientation,`
- `Cinematic photorealistic rendering, ancient Egyptian aesthetic, dramatic underworld lighting, portrait orientation,`

### Vertical Composition Keywords

Include at least one of these to strongly signal vertical framing:

- `portrait orientation`
- `vertical composition`
- `towering [subject] filling the frame`
- `from sky above to [ocean/earth/abyss] below`
- `[subject] rising from below`
- `[element] descending from above`
- `upper third... lower third` (explicit zone description)

### What to Emphasize for LTX-2 Video Prompts

LTX-2 generates motion from a single frame seed. Video prompts should describe:

1. **What is moving** â€” be specific (not "motion in the scene" but "Amaterasu's robes billowing upward")
2. **Direction of motion** â€” upward, downward, spiraling, swaying, cascading
3. **Speed quality** â€” slow motion, gradual, steady, rhythmic
4. **Secondary motion** â€” particles, embers, petals, dust motes, ripples (these fill the frame naturally and look excellent in LTX-2)
5. **Camera behavior** â€” slowly tilting up, camera gently pushing in, wide to medium pull

### What to Avoid in Prompts

- Horizontal panning (wrong axis for portrait)
- Describing audio ("thunderous sound", "roaring ocean") â€” LTX-2 generates visuals only, audio references confuse the model
- Overly complex multi-subject scenes â€” one clear subject with environmental detail works better than crowded compositions
- Landscape keywords ("wide vista", "panoramic view", "horizon stretching left and right")
